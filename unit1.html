1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT has a variety of purposes in modern business. Here are a few examples:

Communications: It enables businesses to connect to other businesses, customers and services. This means a small business can have a global market presence and customer base. Business is no longer geographically restricted like it once was.

Access to Information: Information of all kinds is right at our fingertips. We can access information that is held by others really quickly and easily, we can retrieve small amounts of information from vast pools of information and we can share information with others really easily and quickly. This allows many people, who are geographically very distanced to be able to work on single projects together in real time.

Automation: Business is significantly streamlined by to automation

We are currently in the midst of a global pandemic. If it weren’t for applications like Zoom, many businesses would have been unable to keep afloat and would have collapsed.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Proper and effective planning and a lack of appropriate skills will inevitably make a digital project fail. Similarly, projects that fail to make use of the appropriate tools, methods and resources that a specific project requires will also set it up to fail. Having excellent communications is also essential.

Digital technology is by definition fundamentally fast and bleeding edge. It is hugely competitive and by its very nature, innovative. There is no capacity for people to be slow, to miss deadlines and to produce poor or shoddy work, when so many other teams will be making great use of all the free resources available to everyone and easily producing great results and then being able to deliver them on time too

Digital projects tend to fail due to poor communication, inadequate or ineffective management, scope-creep (the project’s scope expands), ill-defined objectives and badly managed expectations.


According to Enginess Team1, there are 5 main reasons why digital projects fail:

1. Failing to account for business processes
2. Failing to secure all executive buy-in
3. Investing in shiny objects, not customer value
4. Poor definition/expectations setting
5. Poor vendor selection

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

As well as the laws on GDPR, many businesses have their own internal policies and regulations on matters such as information security. The Information Commissioner's Office website is a great resource for guidance and information pertaining to data protection and privacy. This is a very complex and rapidly evolving area of law that cannot be summarised in a single paragraph.

However, GDPR sets out 7 principles:

1. Lawfulness, fairness and transparency
2. Purpose limitation
3. Data minimisation
4. Accuracy
5. Storage limitation
6. Integrity and confidentiality (security)
7. Accountability

GDPR governs online activities in many ways. One example of this is the way in which websites are no longer allowed to add cookies to a user's computer without the user's express agreement.

It is critical for Digital Projects to be GDPR compliant. This will often involve having a working plan for what to do if you are hacked and you will need to act very quickly to report this to the ICO immediately after it has happened as part of a damage limitation exercise. This will help to reduce or completely eliminate any hefty fines being imposed for failing to respond to a data breach.

Businesses need to be mindful of Data Protection law, Health and Safety law, Computer Misuse laws, Equality law (in respect of accessibility, particularly in relation to public bodies and governmental organisations), Copyright infringement, Misuse of Private Information (such as where information is deliberately or negligently leaked by an employee), Defamation and Libel. Failure to comply with GDPR, Health and Safety, Computer Misuse and Equality laws are criminal offences whilst the misuse of private information and defamation are civil wrongs. If you are found to be in breach of these, it is possible that you will be ordered to pay increasingly substantial sums in damages.

3.1 Review the benefits and drawbacks of IT tools and systems used in terms of productivity and efficiency – could you have been more efficient when making your website? How? Could you have done it in teams?

The way in which we work is as individual as we are, hence the abundance of task manager software that is available. Some people prefer to work with pen and paper, and others might prefer to use Gantt charts and sophisticated software. The tools that I might select to use for a project might not be the most suitable for the rest of my team, if they have a different working style to me. If a particular application is selected for a whole team to use, then it is important that the whole team makes good use of it, otherwise for example, if people are not checking off their tasks as being completed when they are, how is the project manager supposed to know what work is and is not done, if the information she is reading is not updated?

In order for people to make proper use of the tools and technology they are expected to use, it is worthwhile ensuring that everyone is sufficiently trained in how to use them appropriately. This can save a lot of time and avoid making errors. Training can be expensive and time-consuming, particularly if you waste time and money training people who already know how to use these technologies. The way in which people learn is also extremely individual.

Creating an open and honest environment in which people feel comfortable about speaking up when mistakes are made and also when improvements have been identified is really important. Establishing a work place culture like this can take a lot of time and investment but the rewards are worth it.

IT tools and systems have proven themselves to be invaluable during the Covid lockdown. They have enabled people to communicate, share files and information and work in a very normal way, even though many have been working from home.

We have had very little time in which to complete this project and therefore it was critical that we maximised out productivity and efficiency. Proper and effective planning has been crucial to this end and I feel that I have achieved this.

Completing this task as a team would not, in my opinion, been a productive task. Completing this task as a team would have required an inordinate amount of planning that we simply did not have time for. For example, a team would have had to agree on all the wording to each and every answer, which would have been extremely time-consuming. Similarly, ensuring the website theme was consistent throughout the website would have also required complete agreement and extensive discussions and planning to achieve this goal. While completing this task as a team is likely to have produced better long-term outcomes, it would not have been achievable in the time frame that we were given to complete the task.
